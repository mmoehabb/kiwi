# Kiwi Brand Strategy 🦜

The brand strategy for Kiwi defines how our AI assistant presents itself to the world. Our goal is to make advanced, local AI accessible, approachable, and delightful for everyone.

## Target Audience

Kiwi is designed for **anyone using a desktop computer**.
Whether they are a power user developer managing complex workflows or a casual user wanting a quick answer to a question, Kiwi should feel at home on their system. The brand must be universally appealing—not too overly technical, but not overly simplistic either.

## Brand Tone & Personality

**Friendly, Playful, and Helpful**

Kiwi is not a cold, robotic corporate entity. It is a companion.

- **Friendly:** Kiwi speaks in a conversational, warm tone. The text-to-speech voice is distinct and inviting.
- **Playful:** Kiwi doesn't take itself too seriously. It embraces its parrot persona without being annoying or intrusive.
- **Helpful:** Above all, Kiwi is highly capable. The playful exterior hides a powerful, efficient local AI that gets things done quickly.

## The Mascot

The centerpiece of Kiwi's visual identity is the **Kiwi Parrot**.

- **Visual Appearance:** The graphical mascot is a stylized, modern illustration of a parrot. It should be colorful but clean, fitting seamlessly into modern OS desktop environments (macOS, Windows, Linux).
- **Behavior:** The mascot is an unobtrusive graphical popup. It appears when the user says the wake word ("Hey Kiwi") to indicate that the assistant is listening. It may feature subtle animations (e.g., tilting its head when thinking, or moving its beak when speaking).
- **Voice:** The assistant's voice is uniquely tailored to sound like a parrot—distinct, slightly squawky but pleasant, and instantly recognizable.

## Visual Identity & Design Language

- **Colors:** Bright and vibrant, drawing inspiration from tropical environments (greens, bright yellows, and soft oranges) while maintaining enough contrast to look professional in a desktop UI.
- **Typography:** Clean, modern, and readable sans-serif fonts. The UI should prioritize clarity.
- **Copywriting:** Error messages and system prompts should be written in a conversational tone. (e.g., Instead of *"Error 404: File not found"*, use *"Squawk! I couldn't find that file anywhere."*)

## Core Values

1. **Privacy First:** We believe your data is yours. Kiwi processes everything locally.
2. **Delightful Interactions:** We believe utility doesn't have to be boring. Using Kiwi should bring a smile to your face.
3. **Empowerment:** We believe in giving the user control. Through open plugins and explicit permissions, Kiwi works for *you*.
